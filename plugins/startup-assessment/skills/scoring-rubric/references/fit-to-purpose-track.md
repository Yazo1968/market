# Fit-to-Purpose Track — Detailed Scoring Guidance

## Overview

The Fit-to-Purpose Track measures whether the content is appropriately constructed for the specific submission context: the identified funding stage, the identified assessor type, and the specific ask (amount, instrument, terms). It answers: "Is this submission built for this assessor, at this stage, requesting this deal?"

This is independent of whether the underlying evidence is complete or high-quality (Readiness Track). A submission can have excellent evidence but be misaligned for a specific context, or vice versa.

Three dimensions:
- **Stage Appropriateness (Dimension C):** Is content pitched at the right level for this stage?
- **Assessor Alignment (Dimension D):** Is content built to address this assessor's analytical priorities?
- **Ask Coherence (Dimension E):** Does the submission collectively justify the specific ask?

All three must be scored independently for each module.

---

## Dimension C — Stage Appropriateness (0–2)

Stage Appropriateness assesses whether the depth, nature, and emphasis of content match the company's identified funding stage. Different stages expect different evidence levels and different narrative framing.

### Score 0 — Misaligned
The content is materially inappropriate for the identified stage. Depth is either excessive (over-engineered detail for early stage) or insufficient (underdeveloped for mature stage). Emphasis or argument style is inconsistent with stage norms.

**When to apply:**
- Pre-seed company presents Series B-level financial modeling with detailed unit economics and 10-year projections
- Series A company presents only MVP description with no traction metrics or customer validation
- Seed company emphasizes founder pedigree and past exits rather than product evidence and initial market validation
- Pre-seed company focuses on regulatory compliance and scale challenges rather than core product-market fit hypothesis
- Growth-stage company is still emphasizing "market validation" rather than competitive positioning and margin expansion

**Examples by Stage:**

**Pre-Seed Misalignment:**
- Market sizing is hyper-detailed (segment-by-segment TAM/SAM/SOM) when stage calls for directional market opportunity hypothesis
- Team section includes advisory board and hiring plans for 20 additional roles when stage should focus on founder expertise and MVP progress
- Financial projections are detailed 10-year models when stage should focus on runway and cash burn assumptions

**Seed Misalignment:**
- Minimal market validation when stage expects 5+ reference customers and quantified early traction
- Deep competitive analysis when focus should be on proof of product-market fit and early adoption signals
- Emphasis on long-term strategic partnerships when stage should focus on immediate product-customer fit

**Series A Misalignment:**
- No customer retention or expansion metrics when stage expects cohort analysis and unit economics
- Competitive positioning is qualitative when stage expects third-party competitive intelligence and market share analysis
- Financial model is high-level "top-down" revenue forecast when stage expects detailed customer-based model with CAC/LTV

**Series B+ Misalignment:**
- Still presenting MVP progress; should be presenting scale metrics, unit economics, and pathway to profitability
- Market size is claimed without sourced, independent validation
- Organizational structure is vague; should include clear org chart, key hires, and scaling plan

### Score 1 — Acceptable
Content is broadly appropriate for the stage but includes elements that are either over-calibrated or under-calibrated. The submission is workable for the stage but could be sharper.

**When to apply:**
- Content includes unnecessary detail for the stage, requiring the assessor to filter signal from noise
- Core elements match stage expectations, but some sections are over- or under-developed
- Emphasis is roughly right but narrative could be more focused on stage-appropriate priorities
- Evidence level is roughly stage-appropriate but with gaps in expected detail or specificity

**Examples by Stage:**

**Pre-Seed Score 1:**
- Market opportunity is described directionally but with more detail than necessary; some high-level TAM/SAM provided but overshadows core MVP hypothesis
- Team section includes some advisor detail beyond what stage requires; focus is appropriate but length is excessive
- MVP is described with good detail, but financial projections are included in brief form (not full models) — slightly over-detailed for stage

**Seed Score 1:**
- Customer validation includes 3 references with good detail; should have 5+; good evidence but under-developed for stage expectations
- Financial projections include P&L but lack unit economics detail (CAC, LTV, retention) expected at stage
- Competitive analysis is present and reasonable but more qualitative than stage norms; some third-party research but mostly company-sourced

**Series A Score 1:**
- Financial model includes detailed pro-forma but lacks explicit cohort-based unit economics; has most detail but could be more granular
- Customer validation is strong but over-emphasizes anecdotal feedback; should emphasize adoption metrics (retention, expansion, NPS)
- Organizational plan is mentioned but not detailed; should include org chart and key hires through runway

**Series B+ Score 1:**
- Scale metrics are presented but missing benchmarking against comparables; good data but could be more contextualized
- Unit economics are detailed but lack full margin expansion pathway; should project to profitability
- Market share analysis is present but lacks specificity; should include specific SAM capture targets

### Score 2 — Well-Calibrated
Content is precisely appropriate in depth, detail, and emphasis for the identified stage. Evidence is at the level expected; emphasis is on stage-appropriate priorities. The assessor does not need to filter or infer.

**When to apply:**
- Content depth and detail match stage expectations exactly
- Narrative emphasis is focused on stage-appropriate priorities
- Evidence level is neither sparse nor excessive; it is "just right" for the stage
- Detail is specific and material, not illustrative or high-level

**Critical Rule:** Score 2 requires that the submission's framing, emphasis, and detail level are optimized for this stage. It is not enough that content *could be relevant*; it must be actively foregrounded and developed.

**Examples by Stage:**

**Pre-Seed Score 2:**
- Market opportunity is described as directional hypothesis with supporting evidence (initial customer conversations, market research signals) and stated as TAM in simple, well-justified form
- MVP is demonstrated or clearly described with feature roadmap tied to customer feedback
- Team bios focus on founder expertise and initial advisors; early hires are planned not detailed
- Financial projections are directional (runway, burn rate, milestone-based spending) not detailed models

**Seed Score 2:**
- Market validation includes 5+ reference customers with documented use cases and adoption metrics (time-to-value, initial retention)
- Financial model includes P&L with explicit unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback period) derived from actual customer cohort data
- Competitive analysis identifies key competitors and supply-side substitutes; includes third-party research on competitive positioning
- Customer acquisition strategy is specific to proven early channels; growth assumptions are grounded in current metrics

**Series A Score 2:**
- Customer validation includes 10+ reference customers with corroborated expansion metrics, churn analysis by cohort, and third-party validation (analyst report, industry award)
- Financial model includes detailed 3–5 year pro-forma with per-customer unit economics, sensitivity analysis, and explicit key assumptions
- Organizational structure includes org chart through target headcount; key hires are specified with role rationale
- Competitive positioning is mapped against specific competitors with third-party intelligence on market share, pricing, and differentiation

**Series B+ Score 2:**
- Scale metrics include quarterly revenue, customer count, payback period, and unit economics by customer segment with benchmarking against comparables
- Financial projections include margin expansion pathway; profitability timeline is explicit
- Organizational structure includes full exec team bios, board composition, and key functional hires with hiring timeline
- Market share analysis includes addressable market, current penetration, and clear pathway to target penetration

## Dimension D — Assessor Alignment (0–2)

Assessor Alignment measures whether the submission is constructed to address the analytical priorities and decision framework of the identified assessor type. Different assessor types (VC, credit, strategic acquirer) have fundamentally different criteria and priorities.

### Score 0 — Misaligned
Content is not constructed to address the identified assessor's analytical priorities. The submission prioritizes concerns that are immaterial to this assessor and neglects concerns that are central.

**When to apply:**
- Submission is optimized for equity investor (valuation, market opportunity, growth) when identified assessor is credit provider (cash flow, collateral, repayment capacity)
- Submission emphasizes market size and competitive positioning when identified assessor is strategic acquirer (acquisition fit, technology IP, team retention)
- Submission is pitched as standalone business when identified assessor is credit provider seeking project finance with specific revenue contracts
- Team section emphasizes diversity and culture when identified assessor is enterprise customer seeking implementation capability and support resources

**Examples by Assessor Type:**

**VC Misalignment (Score 0):**
- Heavy emphasis on profitability and margin management when VC prioritizes market capture and growth
- Minimal discussion of market opportunity; emphasis on current revenue and cost control
- No competitive analysis when VC's thesis requires defensible market position
- MVP-stage product when VC expects clear product-market fit signals

**Credit Provider Misalignment (Score 0):**
- Emphasis on valuation and growth multiples when credit prioritizes cash flow and debt service capacity
- Minimal financial detail; emphasizes market opportunity instead of cash generation
- No discussion of collateral or repayment waterfall
- Revenue assumptions are speculative; no historical data or contracted revenue

**Strategic Acquirer Misalignment (Score 0):**
- Emphasis on TAM and standalone market opportunity when strategic interest is acquisition fit
- Minimal discussion of technology differentiation, IP, or proprietary processes
- Team section emphasizes founder pedigree when strategic interest is technology/talent retention post-acquisition
- No analysis of how offering complements parent company's portfolio

**Enterprise Customer Misalignment (Score 0):**
- Emphasis on market opportunity and scale when customer prioritizes implementation fit and support capability
- Minimal detail on customer success metrics, support resources, or integration support
- No discussion of regulatory compliance or enterprise security requirements
- Team section emphasizes industry experience when customer prioritizes implementation expertise

### Score 1 — Partially Aligned
Content addresses some of the identified assessor's priorities but omits or misdirects on others. The submission is usable but requires the assessor to reframe or fill gaps.

**When to apply:**
- Core narrative is aligned but supporting evidence is optimized for a different assessor type
- Some priorities are addressed; others receive insufficient depth or emphasis
- Content includes some elements of both aligned and misaligned framings
- Assessor can extract relevant information but must filter and reweight substantially

**Examples by Assessor Type:**

**VC Partial Alignment (Score 1):**
- Market opportunity is described with some detail, but emphasis is on current revenue and profitability rather than capture strategy
- Competitive positioning is mentioned but not deeply analyzed
- Customer validation exists but emphasis is on early revenue rather than product-market fit signals
- Financial projections include growth but lack clear margin pathway or unit economics

**Credit Provider Partial Alignment (Score 1):**
- Cash flow projections are included but lack supporting detail on revenue contracts or payment terms
- Balance sheet is presented but asset/liability structure is not optimized for credit analysis
- Some discussion of repayment capacity but emphasis on equity upside rather than debt service
- Financial model includes profitability but debt service coverage ratio is not explicitly calculated

**Strategic Acquirer Partial Alignment (Score 1):**
- Technology differentiation is mentioned but not deeply analyzed against parent's existing capabilities
- Team is described but post-acquisition retention approach is not addressed
- Acquisition fit is implied but not explicitly analyzed
- Customer base is described but overlap with parent's customers is not analyzed

**Enterprise Customer Partial Alignment (Score 1):**
- Implementation roadmap is described but without sufficient detail on support resources or timeline
- Some discussion of compliance/security but not comprehensive against customer's requirements
- Product capability is emphasized but integration complexity is not addressed
- Team includes customer success personnel but resources are understated

### Score 2 — Well-Aligned
Content is directly constructed to address the identified assessor's analytical priorities and decision framework. Emphasis is on the assessor's key decision criteria. Evidence is presented in the form and structure the assessor expects.

**When to apply:**
- Narrative structure mirrors the assessor's decision framework
- Evidence is presented in the metrics and forms the assessor prioritizes
- Emphasis is on assessor-critical factors; tangential concerns are minimized
- Content implicitly guides the assessor through their own analytical process

**Critical Rule:** Score 2 requires intentional, explicit alignment. It is not enough that content *could support* the assessor's decision; it must be actively foregrounded and structured to match their frame.

**Examples by Assessor Type:**

**VC Well-Aligned (Score 2):**
- Market opportunity section presents TAM/SAM/SOM with sourced methodology; growth rates are explicit
- Competitive analysis maps competitive moats and defensible differentiation; positioning is clear against specific competitors
- Customer validation emphasizes product-market fit signals: net expansion rate, cohort retention, customer acquisition efficiency, willingness to pay
- Financial projections include unit economics with CAC/LTV, pathway to profitability, and target stage exit metrics
- Team section emphasizes founder-market fit and startup experience; key hires are aimed at scaling, not specialized expertise

**Credit Provider Well-Aligned (Score 2):**
- Cash flow statement is core; operating cash generation is the first priority, not EBITDA
- Revenue section specifies contracted revenue, payment terms, and customer creditworthiness; speculative revenue is clearly separated
- Balance sheet is organized to highlight asset quality and collateral value
- Debt service coverage ratio is explicitly calculated; repayment waterfall is clear
- Covenant structures are addressed; financial covenants are proposed with realistic buffer
- Scenario analysis includes stress cases: customer loss, revenue decline, extension of payback period

**Strategic Acquirer Well-Aligned (Score 2):**
- Technology section focuses on differentiation relative to parent's existing capabilities and competitive threats to parent's business
- Intellectual property section details patents, proprietary processes, and defensibility against replication
- Team section addresses post-acquisition integration: retention incentives, knowledge transfer, and organizational fit
- Customer analysis highlights overlap with parent's customer base and cross-selling opportunity
- Strategic fit section explicitly articulates how acquisition strengthens parent's competitive position, expands addressable market, or accelerates product roadmap

**Enterprise Customer Well-Aligned (Score 2):**
- Implementation section details phases, resource requirements, timeline to productive use, and support commitment
- Compliance and security section maps requirements to regulatory frameworks and parent company's standards
- Integration section addresses integration with existing systems, data migration, and ongoing support
- Customer success metrics are specified: adoption metrics, usage benchmarks, support ticket resolution targets
- Pricing and contract terms are structured to customer's procurement and budget cycles

## Dimension E — Ask Coherence (0–2)

Ask Coherence measures whether the submission's evidence, arguments, and financial structure collectively and consistently support the specific ask: the amount of capital, the instrument (equity, debt, grant), and the terms (valuation, dilution, interest rate, maturity).

### Score 0 — Incoherent
The submission does not collectively justify the ask. There are material disconnects between the evidence presented and the capital request, or between different sections of the submission.

**When to apply:**
- Requested valuation is inconsistent with presented evidence (e.g., requesting $50M valuation with <$1M current revenue and minimal traction)
- Requested amount (e.g., $5M Series A) cannot be deployed credibly given stated roadmap and burn rate
- Requested terms (e.g., venture debt with 36-month maturity) are misaligned with business model (e.g., 24-month payback period)
- Equity request assumes dilution percentage inconsistent with cap table or post-money valuation
- Use of proceeds does not align with stated priorities or timeline
- Market opportunity described does not justify requested exit multiple or timeline

**Examples of Score 0 — Incoherent:**

**Equity Misalignment:**
- Submission requests $10M Series A at $100M post-money (10% dilution) for company with $100K MRR, no clear growth catalyst, and 5-year payback horizon. No credible case for 10x+ growth to justify valuation.
- Funding request assumes 2-year runway; stated burn rate and headcount plan would consume capital in 18 months. Runway claim is incoherent with plan.

**Debt Misalignment:**
- Requested $2M venture debt at 12% + 2% warrant coverage; stated use is marketing acceleration to reach cash flow breakeven. No clear mechanism to increase cash generation to support debt service.
- Interest-only debt terms are requested; cash flow projections show breakeven is 3 years away. No mechanism to service interest; incoherent.

**Instrument Misalignment:**
- Requesting SAFE or convertible note for Series A round (should be priced equity)
- Requesting revenue-based financing (RBF) when revenue is not yet established
- Requesting venture debt when runway is sub-12 months and dilution is necessary
- Requesting grant when business model is clearly commercial and profitable

**Grant/Strategic Misalignment:**
- Requesting government grant to develop commercial product; grant is intended for research or public benefit, not product commercialization
- Requesting corporate venture investment when strategic fit is minimal; investment thesis is not articulated

### Score 1 — Partially Coherent
The submission supports some elements of the ask but leaves material gaps or inconsistencies.

**When to apply:**
- Evidence supports the capital amount but not the requested valuation or terms
- Evidence supports the capital use but not the capital amount
- Some sections of the submission support the ask; others are missing or vague
- Coherence exists for some use cases but not others
- Financial projections support breakeven timeline but not revenue targets or exit assumptions

**Examples of Score 1 — Partially Coherent:**

**Valuation Gap:**
- Submission presents clear market opportunity ($500M TAM) and customer traction (10 customers, $50K MRR, 20% MoM growth) supporting a $15M Series A request. However, requested valuation of $150M post-money implies 10x+ growth and 50%+ annual margin by Year 3. Traction supports capital amount and deployment, but valuation case is incomplete.

**Deployment Gap:**
- Submission requests $5M for product development, sales hiring, and market expansion. Product roadmap is detailed; sales plan is mentioned. However, specific allocation to each use case and timeline are vague. Assessor must infer deployment credibility.

**Instrument Gap:**
- Submission requests venture debt for working capital and inventory financing. Cash flow supports debt service, but inventory is not being used as collateral and accounts receivable are not pledged. Debt request is partially justified; collateral structure is incomplete.

**Timeline Gap:**
- Submission projects breakeven in 3 years with stated burn rate. Runway calculation is correct for requested capital. However, probability of reaching profitability by that date given current traction is not directly supported. Capital amount is justified; timeline assumptions are partially coherent.

### Score 2 — Coherent
The submission collectively and consistently justifies the specific ask. Capital amount is credibly deployable; terms are commensurate with risk and evidence; all material sections support the ask's core assumptions.

**When to apply:**
- Capital amount aligns with stated use of proceeds and stated timeline to cash flow breakeven or key milestone
- Valuation is justified by presented evidence and stated growth assumptions
- Instrument choice (equity vs. debt vs. hybrid) is appropriate for business model, revenue stage, and financial profile
- Terms (interest rate, warrant coverage, equity dilution, maturity) are commensurate with risk, stage, and payback/exit assumptions
- Financial projections support all material claims: breakeven timeline, revenue targets, margin assumptions, exit assumptions
- Evidence of product-market fit or revenue generation supports the risk profile and terms

**Critical Rule:** Score 2 requires that the submission makes an internally consistent case. The assessor should not need to fill material gaps or resolve inconsistencies.

**Examples of Score 2 — Coherent:**

**Equity Ask:**
- Submission requests $3M Series A for Seed-stage company with $200K MRR, 15% monthly growth, 10+ reference customers, and clear path to $5M ARR in 2 years. Post-money valuation of $20M (15% dilution) is justified by traction and market opportunity. Use of proceeds ($1.5M sales/marketing, $1M product development, $500K operations) is credible for stated growth path. Financial model projects 5x revenue growth and customer expansion. All elements cohere: capital supports stated milestones, milestones support valuation, valuation is justified by traction and opportunity.

**Debt Ask:**
- Submission requests $1.5M venture debt at 12% + 1.5% warrant to support growth capital expenditure. Current cash generation is $200K/month; debt service (principal + interest) is $50K/month. DSCR is 4x, providing conservative margin. Stated use (inventory financing for 3-month demand surge) is credible for collateral. Debt maturity of 48 months aligns with payback period. All elements cohere: business can service debt, collateral is clear, terms reflect risk, no gaps in coherence.

**Hybrid/Strategic:**
- Submission requests $2M strategic partnership with revenue-sharing component (company retains 80% of incremental revenue after partnership fees). Partnership target (enterprise customer integration partnership) is clearly articulated. Revenue model is conservative: $500K Year 1, $2M Year 2, $5M Year 3. Share of upside (20% = $100K, $400K, $1M) provides strategic return while maintaining company incentives. Use of proceeds (dedicated engineering team, integration support, sales support to partner's sales channels) is credible for revenue projection. All elements cohere: partnership terms align with shared value creation; ask size is justified by projected revenue and cost structure.

## Ask Coherence by Ask Type

### Equity Asks

**Seed Round Coherence Checklist:**
- Founder story and market opportunity justify seed-stage investment
- MVP exists and has initial market signals (5+ customers, or 1-2 strong design partners)
- Requested amount covers runway to Series A milestone (product-market fit signals, 10+ customers, $50K+ MRR)
- Post-money valuation is typical for stage (1.5–3x seed round size)
- Team includes at least one founder with relevant startup or market experience

**Series A Coherence Checklist:**
- Product-market fit signals are clear (product-market fit criteria: net expansion rate >130%, customer concentration <30%, retention >90%)
- Traction is substantial ($100K+ MRR, 10+ quality customers, clear cohort retention)
- Requested amount covers 24 months to Series B milestone (scale metrics, $1M+ ARR, unit economics clear)
- Valuation is justified by: (a) revenue multiple (5–10x Series A for SaaS), or (b) clear path to profitability or $10M+ exit
- Use of proceeds is detailed and credible (sales, product, operations)

**Series B+ Coherence Checklist:**
- Unit economics are strong ($3+ LTV/CAC ratio, <12 month payback, >30% gross margin)
- Scale metrics are substantial ($1M+ ARR, clear path to $10M+ ARR)
- Requested valuation reflects milestone achievement and remaining risk
- Use of proceeds is detailed with hiring plan and market expansion strategy
- Path to profitability or Series C milestone is clear

### Debt Asks

**Working Capital Debt Coherence:**
- Current cash generation supports debt service (DSCR >2x)
- Debt maturity aligns with payback period
- Collateral is clearly identified and valued
- Use of proceeds accelerates cash generation (not used for operational burn)

**Growth Debt Coherence:**
- Marginal revenue from capital deployment exceeds debt service cost (payback <18 months)
- Collateral or security is provided (inventory, equipment, or cash flow pledge)
- Company has positive unit economics (margin sufficient to support debt service)
- Debt maturity is reasonable given business cycle (3–5 years typical)

**Project Finance Coherence:**
- Specific project is identified with revenue contracts or clear customers
- Project revenue supports full debt service and returns capital
- Project timeline aligns with debt maturity
- Security is project-specific (customer contracts, equipment, or revenue pledge)

## Scoring Discipline and Common Pitfalls

### Do Not Assume Assessor Context
Always score based on the *stated* assessor type and stage. Do not assume the founder will pivot or the assessor will be flexible.

### Do Not Over-Credit Potential
"We will raise more capital" or "We will hire for scale" does not count toward coherence. Score the current ask and the evidence presented.

### Do Not Penalize Conservative Asks
A lower ask that is clearly coherent scores higher than a higher ask that requires more inference.

### Do Not Conflate Readiness with Fit-to-Purpose
A submission can be complete and high-quality (high Readiness) but poorly aligned for this specific assessor. Score independently.

### Document Coherence Gaps Specifically
"Incoherent" or "undercapitalized" is not rationale. "Requested $5M for Series A; stated burn is $200K/month; runway is 25 months; Series B milestone (product-market fit signals) is expected in 18 months; gap of 7 months with no stated contingency" is.

## Summary Coherence Assessment

| Ask Type | Score 2 Indicators | Score 1 Red Flags | Score 0 Failures |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seed Equity | MVP + 5+ customers, clear runway to Series A, $1.5–2M ask, typical valuation | Some customer signals or traction gaps, vague Series A milestone | No MVP, no customers, unrealistic valuation, misaligned use of proceeds |
| Series A Equity | Clear traction >$100K MRR, 10+ customers, detailed unit economics, credible scaling plan, typical valuation | Good traction but incomplete unit economics, scaling plan is high-level | Insufficient traction, unrealistic valuation, vague use of proceeds |
| Debt | Positive cash flow >2x debt service, clear collateral, <18 month payback on deployed capital | Marginal cash flow coverage, collateral is unclear | Negative cash flow, speculative use, no collateral structure |
| Strategic/Grant | Clear alignment with strategic partner or grantor's goals, mutual value creation, credible deployment | Partial alignment, some gaps in mutual value case | Misalignment, no mutual value, incoherent use |
