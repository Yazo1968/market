# Readiness Track — Detailed Scoring Guidance

## Overview

The Readiness Track measures whether the submission provides sufficient, credible evidence to assess the startup's actual state. It answers: "Do we have enough information, of sufficient quality, to form a judgment?" This is independent of whether that information is well-framed for the specific assessor or context.

Two dimensions:
- **Completeness (Dimension A):** Is the necessary content present?
- **Quality (Dimension B):** Is the content credible and well-founded?

Both must be scored independently for each module.

## Dimension A — Completeness (0–3)

Completeness assesses whether all necessary information for independent analysis is present in the submission. It is **not** about whether the information is presented persuasively or is framed for a specific audience.

### Score 0 — Absent
The content is not present anywhere in the submission.

**When to apply:**
- The module is included in the active framework but the submission contains no relevant content
- No attempt has been made to address this dimension
- The submission explicitly states the information is unavailable

**Examples:**
- Financial projections module requested, but submission contains no pro-forma financials, cash flow forecasts, or numerical targets
- Team credentials module, but no team bios, role descriptions, or background information present
- Product roadmap module, but no timeline, milestones, or development plan stated

**Scoring note:** A score of 0 does not imply fault; it indicates absence. This triggers hard-blocker protocol if the module is hard-blocker-classified.

### Score 1 — Fragmentary
Content is partially present but insufficient to support independent analysis. Pieces may be present in scattered form, or depth is materially inadequate.

**When to apply:**
- Some content is present but critical sub-elements are missing
- Information is present but requires excessive inference or assumption to interpret
- Only anecdotal or illustrative examples are provided; systematic data is absent
- Content exists but is vague, high-level, or lacks supporting detail

**Examples:**
- Market size mentioned as "large TAM" but no quantified market data, addressable market breakdown, or growth assumptions provided
- One or two customer quotes present, but no systematic validation data, use-case evidence, or adoption metrics
- Technology described in marketing language but without technical specification, architectural diagrams, or capability mapping
- Regulatory environment mentioned but no analysis of specific requirements, compliance pathways, or licensing requirements

**Scoring note:** Score 1 indicates the assessor cannot perform independent analysis from this evidence alone. Material gaps require either inference or external research.

### Score 2 — Present
Content is present and workable, but with material gaps that would require supplementary research or assumptions to fully evaluate.

**When to apply:**
- Core content is present and coherent; gaps are in depth, scope, or detail rather than substance
- The submission provides enough for preliminary analysis, but material questions remain
- Some elements are well-documented; others are sketchy or assumed
- Information is recent and relevant, but coverage is incomplete

**Examples:**
- Market sizing includes addressable market (SAM) with supporting methodology, but serviceable obtainable market (SOM) is not quantified; growth rates stated but not sourced
- Customer validation includes 3–5 reference customers with use cases, but adoption metrics (churn, expansion, time-to-value) are not provided
- Team bios present for core founders, but depth is limited; early employee profiles absent
- Financial model includes pro-forma P&L for 3 years, but detailed assumptions on CAC, LTV, or unit economics are not explicit

**Scoring note:** Score 2 is the most common Completeness score. It indicates the submission is workable but invites supplementary research to fill gaps.

### Score 3 — Complete
All necessary information is present with sufficient depth for independent analysis. Gaps are minor or represent genuinely optional detail.

**When to apply:**
- The submission provides systematic coverage of the module domain
- Information is specific, sourced (internally and/or externally), and current
- Depth is sufficient to permit independent evaluation without additional external research
- The assessor can form a judgment without material assumptions or information gaps

**Examples:**
- Market sizing includes TAM, SAM, SOM with methodology and sources; growth assumptions are quantified and sourced; competitive dynamics are mapped; regulatory barriers are characterized
- Customer validation includes >5 reference customers with documented use cases, adoption metrics (time-to-value, churn, expansion), and evidence of product-market fit
- Team section includes full bios for all active leadership and key employees; relevant experience is documented; gaps in necessary expertise are identified and addressed by advisors or hiring plans
- Financial model includes detailed pro-forma P&L, cash flow, and balance sheet; unit economics are explicit with sensitivity analysis; key assumptions are documented and justified

**Scoring note:** Score 3 does not require perfection or exhaustive depth. It indicates the submission is substantially complete and permits independent judgment.

## Dimension B — Quality (0–2)

Quality assesses the credibility and reliability of the content. High-quality content is well-sourced, internally consistent, and aligned with externally verified benchmarks. Low-quality content contains unsourced assertions, internal inconsistencies, or contradictions with external evidence.

### Score 0 — Unreliable
The content is internally inconsistent, unsourced, or directly contradicted by external research.

**When to apply:**
- Claims in the submission contradict verifiable external sources (e.g., stated market growth rate contradicts published analyst reports; claimed regulatory requirement does not align with actual regulation)
- Content contains internal logical inconsistencies (e.g., stated addressable market contradicts customer TAM calculation; projected growth rate contradicts stated customer acquisition strategy)
- Assertions are presented without any sourcing, with circular logic, or with reasoning that does not support the conclusion
- The submission claims a fact that external research has disproven or contradicted
- Content relies entirely on unverified or training-derived sources with no live verification

**Critical Rule:** If content is present but sourced entirely from Training-Derived, Unverified, or Conflicted sources, Quality must be scored 0, **even if the content appears plausible or internally consistent.** The source classification is the determining factor.

**Examples:**
- Submission claims TAM of $50B; analyst reports consistently cite $15B. Score 0.
- Submission states "No regulatory licensing required in our core markets"; government registry documents list active licensing requirements. Score 0.
- Submission projects 200% YoY growth based on "aggressive sales expansion"; current customer count and growth rate are inconsistent with this trajectory. Score 0.
- Market validation claimed based on "industry knowledge" with no sources cited and contradicted by published market research. Score 0.

**Scoring note:** A score of 0 does not indicate poor effort. It indicates the evidence base is unreliable for assessment purposes.

### Score 1 — Acceptable
The content is broadly credible but contains assumptions that require challenge or portions that lack independent verification.

**When to apply:**
- Content is logically consistent and internally coherent, but rests on assumptions not explicitly tested or validated
- Sources are present but limited (e.g., single analyst report; submission's own research; one customer reference)
- Content aligns broadly with external benchmarks but includes elements not independently verified
- Assertions are reasonable but not fully sourced; the assessor can form a working hypothesis but should verify critical elements
- Content includes mix of Verified/Corroborated sources and some unverified or training-derived material; the balance tilts credible but incomplete

**Examples:**
- Market sizing methodology is stated and reasonable; TAM is drawn from one analyst report; SOM projection is the company's own estimate with reasonable assumptions but limited external validation. Score 1.
- Customer validation includes 3–4 reference customers with documented use cases; feedback is consistent and positive but interviews are brief and reference selection was company-directed. Score 1.
- Competitive analysis identifies key competitors and their positioning; comparison is qualitative and based on product review rather than systematically sourced competitive intelligence. Score 1.
- Financial projections are reasonable given stated assumptions; assumptions rest partly on industry benchmarks (sourced) and partly on company estimates (not independently verified). Score 1.

**Scoring note:** Score 1 invites challenge. The assessor should be prepared to verify critical assumptions independently.

### Score 2 — Robust
Content is well-sourced, internally consistent, and aligned with externally verified benchmarks. The assessor can rely on the evidence with high confidence.

**When to apply:**
- Sources are authoritative, recent, and independent (not the company's own research)
- Assertions are directly sourced and verifiable
- Content is internally logically consistent and aligns with external benchmarks
- Material claims are corroborated by multiple independent sources
- Methodology is transparent and replicable; sources are cited

**Critical Rule:** Score 2 requires that all material, score-determining content is Verified or Corroborated. Training-Derived or Unverified sources may provide supporting context but cannot be the primary evidence basis.

**Examples:**
- Market sizing is drawn from multiple independent analyst reports (Gartner, IDC, proprietary research) with consistent estimates; growth rates are sourced to published forecasts; TAM/SAM/SOM calculation is shown and reasonable. Score 2.
- Customer validation includes >5 reference customers with documented, third-party-verified use cases; product adoption metrics (churn, expansion, NPS) are provided with supporting evidence; product-market fit signals are corroborated by third-party research or analyst reports. Score 2.
- Regulatory compliance is documented against actual regulatory authority publications and guidance; legal review is evidenced; compliance pathways are demonstrated with specific reference to regulation. Score 2.
- Financial projections include detailed pro-forma with explicit assumptions sourced to published benchmarks (CAC benchmarks from analyst reports, LTV from customer cohort analysis, retention from actual data); sensitivity analysis is provided. Score 2.

**Scoring note:** Score 2 indicates high confidence in the evidence base. The assessor can proceed with the submission's framing as the working baseline.

## Scoring by Module Type and Stage

Completeness and Quality thresholds vary by module and company stage. The following guidance applies these two dimensions to common modules across stages.

### Pre-Seed (Idea/MVP stage)

**Market Validation Module**
- Completeness 3: TAM estimate with methodology; early market signals (press, customer interest, advisory feedback); identified target customer segments
- Completeness 2: TAM reference without explicit methodology; some customer interest documented
- Completeness 1: Market size stated without support; vague reference to "large market"
- Completeness 0: No market information

- Quality 2: TAM estimate sourced to published research or reasonable bottom-up calculation; early signals corroborated (e.g., multiple customer conversations, user testing feedback)
- Quality 1: Market estimate reasonable but not independently sourced; customer signals are anecdotal
- Quality 0: Market size contradicts published sources or is entirely unverified

**Product Module**
- Completeness 3: Prototype or MVP demo; feature roadmap; product-customer fit hypothesis with supporting evidence
- Completeness 2: Working prototype; basic roadmap; product concept is clear
- Completeness 1: Concept described; prototype or wireframes exist but are early-stage
- Completeness 0: No product deliverable or specification

- Quality 2: Prototype is polished and tested with users; roadmap reflects identified customer needs; technology choices are justified
- Quality 1: Prototype is functional but rough; roadmap is preliminary but grounded in customer feedback
- Quality 0: Prototype is non-functional or technology choices are unjustified

**Team Module**
- Completeness 3: Founder/core team bios with relevant experience; identified expertise gaps and hiring/advisory plan; clear role allocation
- Completeness 2: Core founder bios; some role definition; key gaps identified
- Completeness 1: Founder identified with limited background; team gaps not addressed
- Completeness 0: No team information

- Quality 2: Founder experience is directly relevant to problem/market; gaps are explicitly addressed with advisory board or hiring plan
- Quality 1: Founder experience is broadly relevant; team formation is reasonable but expertise coverage is incomplete
- Quality 0: Founder experience is misaligned or expertise gaps are unaddressed

### Seed (MVP with initial traction)

**Market Validation Module**
- Completeness 3: TAM/SAM with sourced methodology; quantified early traction (customer count, revenue, retention); market dynamics analysis
- Completeness 2: TAM/SAM estimate; early traction numbers; market size validated but not comprehensively analyzed
- Completeness 1: TAM stated; traction is anecdotal or incomplete; market analysis is superficial
- Completeness 0: No market validation

- Quality 2: TAM sourced to analyst reports or systematic research; traction is third-party validated (customer references, public milestones); market dynamics align with published research
- Quality 1: TAM estimate is reasonable; traction numbers are internally reported; market dynamics are stated but not independently verified
- Quality 0: TAM contradicts published sources or traction is unverified; market dynamics are contradicted by research

**Customer Validation Module**
- Completeness 3: 5+ reference customers with documented use cases; retention/churn data; expansion evidence; product-market fit signals
- Completeness 2: 3–4 reference customers; documented use cases; retention or expansion signals present
- Completeness 1: 1–2 customer references; use cases vague; retention/churn not documented
- Completeness 0: No customer references or validation

- Quality 2: Customer interviews are detailed and corroborated; retention/churn is tracked with actual data; expansion is documented with specific examples
- Quality 1: Customer feedback is positive but interviews are brief; retention/churn is estimated; expansion is claimed but not fully documented
- Quality 0: Customer references cannot be verified or retention/churn contradicts claimed traction

### Series A (Product-market fit with scaling)

**Financial Module**
- Completeness 3: Detailed 3–5 year pro-forma (P&L, cash flow, balance sheet); unit economics with cohort analysis; sensitivity analysis; stated assumptions
- Completeness 2: Pro-forma P&L and cash flow; unit economics stated; some assumptions documented
- Completeness 1: High-level financial summary; projections stated without supporting calculation; assumptions vague
- Completeness 0: No financial projections

- Quality 2: Assumptions sourced to market benchmarks (CAC from industry reports, retention from comparable companies); unit economics are derived from actual customer data; projections are stress-tested
- Quality 1: Assumptions are reasonable but not independently sourced; unit economics are estimated from partial data
- Quality 0: Assumptions contradict market benchmarks or unit economics are unsupported

**Competitive Module**
- Completeness 3: Competitive landscape mapped (direct competitors, adjacencies, substitutes); competitive positioning matrix; differentiation articulation
- Completeness 2: Key competitors identified; some positioning analysis; differentiation stated
- Completeness 1: Competitors mentioned; positioning is high-level; differentiation is vague
- Completeness 0: No competitive analysis

- Quality 2: Competitive intelligence sourced to third-party research or databases; positioning is based on product analysis or published materials; differentiation is validated by customer feedback
- Quality 1: Competitor research is company-sourced; positioning is based on product review; differentiation is claimed but not validated
- Quality 0: Competitive analysis contradicts published sources or differentiation is unsubstantiated

## Edge Cases and Special Situations

### Content Present but Entirely Unverified

**Rule:** If content is present (Completeness 1–3) but sourced entirely from Training-Derived, Unverified, or Conflicted sources, Quality must be scored 0.

**Example:**
- Submission includes market sizing analysis based on general industry knowledge but cites no sources and cannot be independently verified.
- Financial projections are presented without assumption sources; assumptions are reasonable but cannot be verified to market benchmarks.
- Competitive analysis is described but drawn only from company's own product knowledge and marketing materials, not from independent competitive research.

In all these cases: Completeness may be 1–3 (content is present), but Quality is 0.

### Internally Consistent but Contradicted by Research

**Rule:** If content is internally consistent but contradicted by accessible, authoritative external sources, Quality is 0.

**Example:**
- Submission claims $200M TAM; three independent analyst reports cite $50–75M. Quality 0.
- Submission states "no licensing required"; regulatory authority documentation lists active licensing. Quality 0.

### Research-Resolved Gaps

**Rule:** If the module is scored as "research-resolved" (external research fills material Completeness gaps), only Verified or Corroborated research contributes to Quality scoring.

**Example:**
- Submission includes market TAM without sourcing (Completeness 2). External research locates the source and validates it (Verified). Quality can be 1 or 2 depending on whether additional corroboration exists.
- Submission lacks customer data; external research locates third-party analyst report on product usage (Verified). Quality can be 1 if corroboration is limited, 2 if corroborated by multiple sources.

## Scoring Discipline and Common Pitfalls

### Avoid Subjective Adjustment
Do not adjust Completeness or Quality scores based on how "impressive" the company is or how well-positioned it appears to succeed. Score the evidence, not the company.

### Do Not Trade Off Dimensions
Completeness and Quality are independent. Do not allow high Completeness to inflate Quality scoring or vice versa.

### Do Not Anticipate Founder Promises
If the submission states "we will hire for expertise" or "we plan to conduct market research," do not credit the gap as complete. Score the current state.

### Do Not Blend Submission and Research
Distinguish between what the submission provides and what external research provides. The Research Protocol determines eligibility of research for Quality scoring.

### Document Every Score
Every raw score must be accompanied by specific, traceable rationale. "Well presented" is not rationale; "TAM estimate is sourced to Gartner and IDC reports with consistent $50B figure" is.

---

## Calibration Notes by Stage

**Pre-Seed:** Expect lower Completeness overall (1–2 range is normal). Quality focus is on coherence and avoiding internal contradiction, not comprehensive external validation.

**Seed:** Expect Completeness 2–3 on core modules. Quality should reflect early external validation (customer references, initial traction).

**Series A:** Expect Completeness 3 on most modules. Quality should reflect robust external sourcing and corroboration.

**Series B+:** Expect Completeness 3 and Quality 2 on most modules. Any lower scores require explicit justification.
