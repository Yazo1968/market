# Startup Assessment Plugin - Reference Files

This directory contains five comprehensive reference files for scoring agents evaluating startup pitches across the core investment decision domains.

## Files Overview

### Domain 1: Market and Opportunity (`domain-1-market.md`)
**Focus**: Market validation, competitive positioning, demand proof
- 8 modules: Problem Definition, Market Sizing, Market Dynamics, Timing Thesis, Competitive Landscape, Competitive Positioning, Customer Segmentation, Demand Validation
- **397 lines**: Detailed guidance on assessing market attractiveness and demand validation

### Domain 2: Solution and Product (`domain-2-product.md`)
**Focus**: Product viability, development stage, technology soundness
- 8 modules: Solution Description, Value Proposition, Development Stage, Technology Architecture, Intellectual Property, Product Roadmap, Defensibility Assessment, Regulatory/Compliance Profile
- **400 lines**: Instructions for evaluating product quality, development stage credibility, and technical feasibility

### Domain 3: Business Model (`domain-3-business-model.md`)
**Focus**: Revenue model sustainability, unit economics, profitability path
- 8 modules: Revenue Model, Pricing Strategy, Cost Structure, Unit Economics (CAC/LTV/Payback), Operating Leverage, Revenue Quality, Customer Economics, Monetization Sequencing
- **400 lines**: Detailed assessment of financial model viability and sustainable growth mechanics

### Domain 4: Go-to-Market (`domain-4-gtm.md`)
**Focus**: Customer acquisition strategy, sales efficiency, market entry approach
- 8 modules: Sales Strategy, Channel Strategy, Marketing Strategy, Customer Acquisition Economics, Retention and Expansion, Partnership Strategy, Market Entry Sequencing, Pipeline and Backlog
- **404 lines**: Framework for evaluating go-to-market execution and customer acquisition sustainability

### Domain 5: Traction (`domain-5-traction.md`)
**Focus**: Proof of business model viability through real-world metrics
- 8 modules: Revenue History, Customer Metrics, Product Engagement, Commercial Milestones, Pilot/PoC Results, Independent Validation, Failure and Adaptation, Sustainability Assessment
- **403 lines**: Guidance on evaluating actual traction metrics and business validation

## Total Content
- **2,004 lines** of substantive, imperative guidance
- **40 detailed modules** (8 per domain)
- **Comprehensive cross-domain dependency notes** linking evaluation contexts

## Usage for Scoring Agents

Each reference file contains a standardized structure:

1. **Module Definition**: Clear explanation of what the module evaluates
2. **What Good Looks Like**: Specific indicators of strong evidence
3. **What Weak/Absent Looks Like**: Red flags and failure patterns
4. **Key Questions**: Critical questions to answer during assessment
5. **What to Look For**: Specific submission elements that constitute proof
6. **Cross-Domain Dependencies**: How this domain informs others
7. **Assessment Notes**: Agent-specific guidance for rigorous evaluation

## Key Assessment Principles

- **No placeholder text**: Every section contains specific, actionable guidance
- **Imperative voice**: Written as direct instructions for AI scoring agents
- **Evidence-grounded**: All guidance is based on standard investment evaluation practices
- **Realistic benchmarks**: Includes industry standard metrics and comparables
- **Explicit red flags**: Lists specific indicators of risk or concern
- **Interconnected**: Cross-references show how domains validate each other

## Implementation Notes

These references are designed to be consumed by AI scoring agents evaluating:
- Narrative pitch materials
- Financial models and projections
- Product demos and technical documentation
- Customer testimonials and case studies
- Traction metrics and historical data

The files provide the scoring agent with comprehensive analytical frameworks for:
- Identifying what constitutes credible evidence
- Assessing the quality and realism of claims
- Detecting inconsistencies across domains
- Benchmarking against industry standards
- Determining investment risk and upside potential
