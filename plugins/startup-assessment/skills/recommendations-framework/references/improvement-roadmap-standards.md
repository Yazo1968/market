# Improvement Roadmap Standards: Path A Construction Guide

This reference file provides standards for building professional, evidence-based improvement roadmaps in Path A recommendations.

---

## 1. Gap Prioritization Framework

### Hard Blocker Gaps (Priority 1)

Hard blocker gaps are critical issues that must be resolved before any re-assessment makes sense. The company cannot meaningfully improve its determination without addressing these gaps first.

#### Characteristics
- Regulatory non-compliance or violation (not just future compliance risk)
- Missing or dysfunctional founder team (e.g., all co-founders departed, founder unable to lead)
- Failed unit economics at current scale (unit loss increases with scale, not decreases)
- Fundamental product failure (cannot build product that customers want)
- No customer traction despite 12+ months of effort (zero customers, zero revenue after extensive attempt)
- Inadequate capital for stated business model (burn rate exceeds fundraising capacity)

#### Timeline
- 30 days or urgent (as applicable to the specific blocker)
- Most hard blockers are binary: either resolved or not

#### Examples
- "Company operating without required FCA license in UK market; non-compliant as of assessment date. Founder must apply for full authorization within 30 days to continue operations."
- "Both co-founders departed in Q3 2025; founder CEO remaining solo with no CFO/COO. Must hire COO or CFO within 60 days; role must be hands-on operational partner."
- "Unit economics: CAC of $400, LTV of $200 at current pricing. Economics fail at any scale. Must either (1) raise prices 50%+ with customer interview validation, or (2) pivot business model to reduce CAC by 60%+. Cannot proceed to Series A discussion without resolution."

#### Remediation Action (Hard Blocker Example)
"Engage recruitment firm specializing in COO/CFO hires in [vertical]. Target: hire by end of Q2 2025. Candidate must have 10+ years operational experience in [similar stage company]. Deliverable: signed employment agreement with start date <90 days from hire date."

#### Impact on Determination
"This is a blocking issue. Closing this gap (hiring operational co-founder, achieving unit economics viability, or obtaining regulatory compliance) is a prerequisite to re-assessment. Current determination cannot improve without resolution. If closed, re-assessment likely to increase determination from [current] to [projected], subject to reassessment of other gaps."

---

### High-Severity Gaps (Priority 2)

High-severity gaps directly impact the assessment determination. Closure would improve the overall score and potentially improve the determination from PASS to HOLD, HOLD to CONDITIONAL GO, or CONDITIONAL GO to GO.

#### Characteristics
- Materially weak validation on critical domain (e.g., market sizing based on no research, customer validation based on friends/family only)
- Significant team gaps (e.g., no CFO at Series A, no VP Sales at revenue scale, advisor board mostly paper-based)
- Missing financial or operational controls (no monthly financials, no board meetings, no HR policies)
- Unproven customer acquisition or retention model (CAC/LTV not measured, churn not tracked)
- Unresolved major risk (competitor entering market, key customer concentration, regulatory uncertainty)

#### Timeline
- 30–90 days

#### Examples
- "Market sizing based on $2.5B TAM assumption not validated by independent research. Assumption relies on single Gartner report; actual addressable market for SMB segment in North America untested against customer interviews."
- "Customer retention unpredictable: no monthly churn tracking; no cohort analysis; founder reports 'customers stay', but no data to support claim. At 50% customer concentration (top customer = 40% of revenue), churn of key customer would be catastrophic."
- "Team: Founder CEO, Founder CTO, 2 engineers. No CFO, no VP Sales, no operations person. Company at $1M ARR but lacks financial controls (no monthly close, no board meetings, no strategic planning process)."

#### Remediation Action (High-Severity Example)
"Commission an independent market sizing study from a Tier 1 research firm (e.g., Gartner, IDC, Arthur D. Little) covering the addressable market for [specific vertical/geography]. Budget: $15K–$25K. Study must define TAM/SAM/SOM with bottom-up customer segment analysis. Conduct 15–20 customer depth interviews validating TAM assumptions. Combine expert research + customer interviews into final report."

#### Impact on Determination
"Closing this gap would increase the Market domain score from approximately 6/10 to 8/10 (with validated independent research). This would improve the overall readiness score by approximately 15–20 points, moving determination from [current] to [projected if only this gap closed]. Impact subject to re-assessment; other gaps may prevent determination improvement."

---

### Medium-Severity Gaps (Priority 3)

Medium-severity gaps improve the relative standing within the current determination category but don't fundamentally change the determination. They should be addressed within 90–180 days.

#### Characteristics
- Additional validation supporting current thesis (more customer references, expanded market research, additional market entry validation)
- Process improvements and formalization (board governance structure, reporting cadence, HR formalization)
- Incremental team improvements (advisor additions, fractional CFO, subject matter expert board member)
- Efficiency improvements (capital efficiency metrics tracking, retention improvements, operational metrics)

#### Timeline
- 90–180 days

#### Examples
- "Governance: no formal board structure; no regular board meetings. Company at Series A stage needs quarterly board meetings, board consent resolutions, and governance documentation."
- "Traction: company has 10 customers, $250K ARR, but customer concentration risk: top 3 customers = 60% of revenue. Need to expand customer base to 20+ customers with more balanced revenue mix."
- "Operations: no documented processes; no financial controls; no HR policies. Company needs monthly financial close, quarterly reporting package, and documented hiring/termination procedures."

#### Remediation Action (Medium-Severity Example)
"Establish formal board governance structure: (1) schedule quarterly board meetings (minimum 4 per year), (2) adopt standard board consent resolutions and documentation, (3) hire fractional CFO (0.5–1 day/week) to prepare monthly financials and quarterly board deck, (4) formalize board composition with 1 independent advisor. Timeline: 3–4 months."

#### Impact on Determination
"Addressing this gap would improve governance and operations scoring, demonstrating professional management and investor-grade controls. Closing this gap would increase Operations domain score from 5/10 to 7/10. This would improve overall readiness by approximately 10 points, strengthening the company's position within the [current determination] category. Re-assessment would likely maintain or modestly improve determination (if no other gaps are blocking)."

---

### Low-Severity Gaps (Priority 4)

Low-severity gaps are nice-to-have improvements that address peripheral weaknesses but are not critical to the investment decision. These should be addressed at the next major milestone (fundraising round, market entry, product launch).

#### Characteristics
- Nice-to-have process improvements (formalized recruiting, expanded advisory board, enhanced reporting)
- Incremental validation (additional market research, case studies, analyst coverage)
- Peripheral team additions (CFO vs. fractional CFO, dedicated HR person)

#### Timeline
- 180+ days or at next major milestone

#### Examples
- "Advisory board: currently 2 informal advisors; could expand to 4–5 advisors with specific domain expertise (go-to-market, product, vertical market experience)."
- "Brand / market presence: limited analyst coverage, no press mentions, no thought leadership. Could enhance market perception through PR, conference speaking, analyst reports."

#### Remediation Action (Low-Severity Example)
"Recruit 2–3 additional advisors with specific domain expertise in [vertical/go-to-market]. Each advisor commits to 4 hours/quarter; advises on strategy, makes introductions, provides subject matter expertise. Timeline: 4–6 months; ideally completed before Series A close."

#### Impact on Determination
"This is a strengthening factor, not a determining factor. Closing this gap would enhance the investor-grade profile and de-risk execution risk perception. Estimated impact: minimal on overall score (2–3 points), but meaningful in positioning for fundraising and investor confidence. Optional; not critical to determination."

## 2. Evidence Standards by Domain

The evidence required to close a gap must be specific, verifiable, and demonstrate credible remediation. Below are standards for each of the 10 assessment domains.

### Market Domain Evidence Standards

#### Market Sizing Gap
- **Evidence**: independent market research report (Gartner, IDC, Arthur D. Little, McKinsey, Bain)
- **Content**: TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown by customer segment, geography, use case
- **Validation**: report issued within 12 months; cites primary sources (customer surveys, industry data)
- **Cost**: $15K–$40K for comprehensive study
- **Timeline**: 6–12 weeks

#### Market Validation Gap
- **Evidence**: documented customer depth interviews (10–20 customers minimum)
- **Content**: interview notes, willingness-to-pay validation, pain point confirmation
- **Format**: written interview summary per customer; unbiased note-taking (third-party interviewer preferred)
- **Validation**: customers represent target segment; conducted at arms-length (not friends/family)
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks

#### Competitive Landscape Gap
- **Evidence**: competitive landscape report analyzing 5–10 competitors
- **Content**: feature comparison, pricing comparison, market positioning, differentiation
- **Validation**: report current within 6 months; includes both direct and indirect competitors
- **Timeline**: 4–6 weeks

### Customer Domain Evidence Standards

#### Customer Reference Gap
- **Evidence**: documented customer reference conversations (minimum 3 reference customers)
- **Content**: customer name, company, use case, implementation outcome, NPS / satisfaction
- **Format**: customer reference form signed by customer reference (with permission to share)
- **Validation**: references from different customer segments (not all same vertical); not cherry-picked happy customers (include some neutral references)
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks

#### Customer Concentration Gap
- **Evidence**: customer list with revenue breakdowns (top 10 customers, revenue per customer)
- **Format**: spreadsheet showing customer name, ARR, % of total revenue, contract renewal date
- **Validation**: audited by third-party accountant (if revenue >$1M) or founder certification
- **Timeline**: 2–4 weeks (already available internally)

#### Customer Acquisition / Retention Gap
- **Evidence**: 6–12 months of monthly customer and revenue cohort data
- **Content**: monthly new customers, monthly churn, monthly ARR change, CAC, LTV, payback period
- **Format**: Excel model with monthly cohorts and trailing 12-month metrics
- **Validation**: audited by accountant or third-party analyst; source data from accounting system
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks to compile and audit

### Product Domain Evidence Standards

#### Product-Market Fit Gap
- **Evidence**: customer usage data demonstrating product stickiness
- **Metrics**: monthly active users, session frequency, feature adoption, NPS score >50
- **Format**: product dashboard, Mixpanel/Amplitude report, customer survey results
- **Validation**: data audited by third-party; represents actual usage not vanity metrics
- **Timeline**: 4–6 weeks (if data already collected, 2 weeks to compile)

#### Product Roadmap / Completeness Gap
- **Evidence**: documented product roadmap (12–18 month view)
- **Content**: features planned, customer problems addressed, timeline, resource plan
- **Format**: written roadmap document; communicated to board and key customers
- **Validation**: roadmap based on customer feedback (not founder assumptions); realistic resource plan
- **Timeline**: 2–4 weeks to document

#### Feature / Technical Gap
- **Evidence**: proof-of-concept or production implementation of missing feature
- **Content**: feature working in production; customer using feature; usage metrics showing adoption
- **Format**: demo, customer case study, product metrics
- **Validation**: feature meets customer requirements; no major bugs
- **Timeline**: 6–12 weeks (engineering effort dependent)

### Unit Economics Domain Evidence Standards

#### CAC / LTV Gap
- **Evidence**: 6–12 months of transaction-level data showing CAC and LTV calculations
- **Content**: monthly cohort data; CAC calculation by channel; LTV by cohort; payback period
- **Format**: audited Excel model with month-by-month detail
- **Validation**: third-party audit; source data from accounting + product system
- **Definition**: clear definition of CAC (marketing + sales cost / new customers), LTV (gross profit * average lifetime / monthly churn)
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks to compile; 2 weeks for third-party audit

#### Gross Margin Gap
- **Evidence**: 6–12 months of P&L showing COGS, revenue, and gross margin
- **Content**: monthly COGS, monthly revenue, monthly gross margin %
- **Format**: audited P&L from accounting system
- **Validation**: third-party audit of cost allocation; clear definition of what counts as COGS
- **Timeline**: 2–4 weeks (accounting should have this monthly)

#### Unit Economics Improvement Path
- **Evidence**: forward projection showing path to positive unit economics
- **Content**: pricing changes, cost structure changes, volume changes needed to achieve 3:1 LTV:CAC
- **Format**: financial model with assumptions documented
- **Validation**: assumptions tied to concrete actions (e.g., "pricing increase 20% based on customer willingness-to-pay study")
- **Timeline**: 4–6 weeks to develop and validate

### Team Domain Evidence Standards

#### Executive / Functional Hire Gap
- **Evidence**: signed employment agreement or offer letter for new executive
- **Content**: name, role, start date, compensation, background
- **Format**: official employment agreement + new hire announcement
- **Validation**: candidate has relevant experience (10+ years in relevant domain); reference checks completed
- **Timeline**: 6–12 weeks (executive recruiting typically takes 8–12 weeks)

#### Advisor Addition Gap
- **Evidence**: signed advisor agreement with new advisor
- **Content**: advisor name, expertise, commitment (hours/quarter), term
- **Format**: formal advisor agreement; public announcement or confidential
- **Validation**: advisor has relevant expertise; existing advisors recommend
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks

#### Team Capability Gap
- **Evidence**: team training completion or certification
- **Content**: founder/team member completed relevant certification, bootcamp, or training program
- **Format**: certificate of completion, transcript, or employer certification
- **Examples**: MBA for founder lacking business education; sales certification for VP Sales; product management certification
- **Timeline**: 6–12 weeks (depending on program)

### Operations Domain Evidence Standards

#### Financial Controls Gap
- **Evidence**: audited or reviewed financial statements (monthly, quarterly)
- **Content**: audited P&L, balance sheet, cash flow for most recent 6–12 months
- **Format**: financial statements prepared by third-party accountant (not founder)
- **Validation**: audit performed by licensed CPA; includes notes and reconciliations
- **Timeline**: 4–6 weeks for initial audit; then monthly ongoing

#### Board Governance Gap
- **Evidence**: board meeting minutes, board resolutions, board composition documentation
- **Content**: quarterly board meeting minutes, signed board consents, formal board composition (names, roles)
- **Format**: official board documentation; maintained by corporate counsel
- **Validation**: board meetings held quarterly; board resolutions signed by all directors
- **Timeline**: 2–4 weeks to establish; then ongoing

#### HR / People Processes Gap
- **Evidence**: documented HR policies, hiring processes, employee handbook
- **Content**: hiring process documentation, offer letter template, employee handbook, termination policy
- **Format**: HR manual; policies distributed to all employees
- **Validation**: policies compliant with local employment law; communicated to all staff
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks to develop and formalize

### Traction Domain Evidence Standards

#### Revenue Traction Gap
- **Evidence**: 6–12 months of monthly revenue data
- **Content**: monthly revenue growth rate, annual recurring revenue (if SaaS), gross margin
- **Format**: audited P&L showing monthly revenue line
- **Validation**: third-party audit; reconciled to accounting system
- **Timeline**: 2–4 weeks to compile

#### Customer Traction Gap
- **Evidence**: monthly customer addition data and customer list
- **Content**: monthly new customer additions, monthly churn, net customer growth
- **Format**: audited customer list; monthly metrics dashboard
- **Validation**: third-party audit of customer count; actual usage verification
- **Timeline**: 2–4 weeks

### Risk / Compliance Domain Evidence Standards

#### Regulatory Compliance Gap
- **Evidence**: regulatory approval letter, license, or compliance certification
- **Content**: official regulatory approval from competent authority (FCA, SAMA, SEC, etc.)
- **Format**: official government letter or certificate
- **Validation**: approval from recognized regulatory body; dated within 6 months
- **Timeline**: 90–180 days (regulatory approval timelines vary significantly)

#### IP / Legal Gap
- **Evidence**: IP audit report and IP assignment agreements
- **Content**: patent applications, trademark registrations, copyright registrations, employee IP assignments
- **Format**: official IP audit report from qualified IP counsel
- **Validation**: audit confirms clear IP ownership; no encumbrances; filed with appropriate registries
- **Timeline**: 4–8 weeks

#### Insurance / Liability Gap
- **Evidence**: insurance policies and coverage verification
- **Content**: general liability, professional liability, cyber insurance, D&O insurance (if Series A+)
- **Format**: insurance declarations page; coverage verification letter from insurer
- **Validation**: policies active and current; coverage limits appropriate for company stage
- **Timeline**: 2–4 weeks

### Fit Domain Evidence Standards

#### Founder-Market Fit Gap
- **Evidence**: founder background documentation and customer testimonials
- **Content**: founder background in similar markets or problems; customer quote on founder credibility
- **Format**: founder bio, customer testimonial, industry recognition
- **Validation**: customers acknowledge founder's credibility; third-party verification (industry interviews)
- **Timeline**: 4–6 weeks to gather testimonials

#### Business Model Fit Gap
- **Evidence**: business model validation through customer pilots or partnerships
- **Content**: signed LOI or pilot agreement with customer; pilot results documented
- **Format**: customer testimonial; pilot case study; commercial agreement
- **Validation**: pilot with real customer; measurable outcomes
- **Timeline**: 8–12 weeks (pilot execution)

## 3. Remediation Action Writing Standards

### Be Specific

Bad: "Improve market research"
Good: "Commission an independent market sizing study from Gartner or IDC covering the addressable market for supply chain analytics in North America. Budget: $20K. Deliverable: TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown by customer segment, with bottom-up validation against 10+ customer interviews. Timeline: 8–10 weeks."

### Be Actionable

Bad: "Increase team capabilities"
Good: "Hire a VP Sales with 8+ years of SaaS sales experience and track record of scaling sales org from $500K to $5M ARR. Recruit through specialized executive search firm (e.g., Seismic Search, Heidrick & Struggles). Timeline: 12 weeks from search kickoff to hire start date."

### Be Measurable

Bad: "Improve unit economics"
Good: "Execute pricing increase from $500/month to $600/month (20% increase). Validate pricing with customer interviews (5–10 customers across key segments). Measure impact: track monthly CAC and LTV for 2 cohorts post-price increase. Target: achieve 3:1 LTV:CAC ratio within 6 months. Success metric: gross margin improves from 70% to 75%+."

### Be Time-Bound

Bad: "Secure customer references"
Good: "Secure 3–5 customer references from paying customers (ARR >$10K, 6+ months as customer). References must cover different use cases (e.g., 1 enterprise, 2 mid-market). Deliver written customer reference forms with customer names, company, specific use cases, and willingness to be contacted by investors. Timeline: 6 weeks."

### Avoid Founder-Unfriendly Language

Bad: "The founder must immediately hire a competent CFO or risk the company's future."
Good: "The company should hire or contract with a CFO/Controller to establish monthly financial close, quarterly reporting, and board-ready financial statements. This will provide the financial controls needed to support Series A discussions. Timeline: 3–4 months to hire and onboard."

## 4. Impact on Determination Language: Templates

### Template for Hard Blocker Gap Impact

"This is a blocking issue. The company cannot improve its assessment outcome without addressing this gap. Current determination of [PASS/HOLD/CONDITIONAL GO] reflects this critical gap. 

If this gap is closed (specifically: [describe remediation]), re-assessment would likely result in determination improvement to [projected determination]. However, re-assessment is conditional on successful remediation and will be subject to verification of the closure and re-evaluation of all other domains.

Timeline: [timeframe] to close this gap."

### Template for High-Severity Gap Impact

"Closing this gap would materially improve the [Domain] domain score. Current Domain score: [X]/10 (reflects gap in [specific area]). 

If gap is closed (by [remediation action]), Domain score would likely increase to [X+3 to X+5]/10. This would improve overall readiness score by approximately [N] points, moving from [current total score] to [projected total score].

Current determination is [PASS/HOLD/CONDITIONAL GO]. With this gap closed alone, determination might improve to [projected determination], conditional on re-assessment and assuming all other domain scores remain constant. However, actual determination improvement depends on status of other high-severity and blocker gaps.

Recommended timeline to close: [weeks/months]."

### Template for Medium-Severity Gap Impact

"Closing this gap would strengthen the company's position within the current [determination] category. 

Current [Domain] domain score: [X]/10. If this gap is closed, expected improvement to [X+2 to X+3]/10. This would contribute [5–10] points to overall readiness score.

Determination impact: minimal direct impact on current determination, but closing this gap demonstrates professional management and investor-grade controls, which strengthens investor confidence. Likely maintains current [determination] while improving relative strength of that determination category.

Timeline: [weeks/months]."

### Template for Low-Severity Gap Impact

"This gap represents a nice-to-have improvement that is not critical to the investment decision but would strengthen the overall profile.

Impact: [minimal], estimated [2–3] point improvement to overall readiness score. Determination impact: none (does not change determination), but enhances investor perception of team quality and strategic positioning.

Recommended timeline: address at next major milestone (next fundraising round, product launch, market entry)."

### Language to Avoid

- "Guaranteed to improve": say "likely to improve" or "would improve" (re-assessment is conditional)
- "Will result in GO": say "might improve to GO" or "could support GO if all other factors align"
- "Definitely": always use "likely", "approximately", "probably", or similar conditional language
- "Must": use "should" or "recommended" (softer tone)

### Standard Disclaimer Language (Boilerplate)

Always include at end of each impact statement:

"Impact statement assumes re-assessment under current market conditions. Actual outcome is subject to full re-assessment and may differ based on new evidence, market changes, or other factors outside remediation of this specific gap. Submitter should view this roadmap as a professional assessment of material opportunities to strengthen the investment case, not as a guarantee of improved determination."

## 5. Tone and Professional Standards

### Constructive, Not Punitive

Bad: "The founder has demonstrated poor execution and lacks business acumen. The company has failed to validate its market, wasted capital on a failed product pivot, and cannot manage basic HR processes."

Good: "The company has made progress on core product development and customer adoption. The following roadmap addresses areas where additional validation and operational maturity will strengthen the investment case: (1) independent market sizing, (2) formal financial controls, (3) expanded customer reference base."

### Acknowledge Strengths

Always open the roadmap with a strengths summary:

Good: "The [Company] team has demonstrated clear execution capability: [specific traction metric]. The product has achieved [specific traction]. Customer feedback indicates [specific validation]. The following roadmap addresses gaps in [specific areas] that will strengthen the overall investment case."

### Frame Gaps as Opportunities

Bad: "The company is weak in operations and governance."

Good: "The company has growth momentum and can strengthen its operational foundation through formalized board governance, monthly financial reporting, and documented HR processes. These improvements will position the company for Series A fundraising."

### Avoid Jargon / Explain Technical Terms

Bad: "Unit economics exhibit poor DSCR; CAC payback exceeds 18 months; LTV:CAC ratio is inverted."

Good: "Unit economics currently show:
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC): $400 per customer
- Customer lifetime value (LTV): $300 per customer
- Payback period: 18 months

This means the company loses money on each customer in the short term and recovers that investment over 18 months. To support sustainable growth, the company should target a 3:1 LTV:CAC ratio (each customer generates $3 in lifetime profit per $1 acquisition cost)."

### Use Conditional, Not Absolute Language

Bad: "The company will improve to GO if these gaps are closed."

Good: "If these gaps are closed, re-assessment would likely improve the determination from PASS to HOLD or CONDITIONAL GO. Actual outcome is subject to re-assessment and depends on status of all gaps."

### Avoid Judgment of Team

Bad: "The founder is not investor-ready. The team lacks fundraising experience."

Good: "The company will benefit from professional governance and board oversight as it scales. Adding a CFO and formalizing board meetings will position the team for Series A fundraising and professional investor partnerships."

### Professional Tone Throughout

- Respectful to founder
- Objective analysis (not opinion)
- Forward-looking (what to fix, not what went wrong)
- Actionable guidance (specific steps, not vague suggestions)
- Realistic timelines (not aggressive or overly optimistic)

## 6. Roadmap Grouping and Presentation

### Group Related Gaps When Remediation Overlaps

Example Grouping:
- **Market Validation Phase** (8–12 weeks)
  - Commission independent market sizing study (TAM/SAM/SOM)
  - Conduct 15–20 customer depth interviews validating market thesis
  - Gather 3–5 customer references across target segments
  - (All three gaps closed by single "market validation phase")

- **Team / Operational Maturity Phase** (10–14 weeks)
  - Hire VP Sales (8–12 weeks recruiting + onboarding)
  - Establish formal board governance (4–6 weeks)
  - Hire/contract fractional CFO (4 weeks onboarding)
  - (All three gaps closed through parallel operational initiatives)

### Present in Priority Order

1. Hard blocker gaps first
2. High-severity gaps second
3. Medium-severity gaps third
4. Low-severity gaps fourth (if included; often omitted if list is long)

### Use Clear Visual Hierarchy

- **Roadmap Phase 1**: Market Validation (timeline, gaps closed, resources needed)
- **Roadmap Phase 2**: Team / Operational Build (timeline, gaps closed, resources needed)
- **Roadmap Phase 3**: Financial Controls & Governance (timeline, gaps closed, resources needed)
